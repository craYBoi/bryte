The 3 most important things about a great headshot photo

A great headshot photo always looks natural.

First, Lighting

Lighting means the lighting on the person’s face, it comes from Key light. In a good headshot photo:

  1. The person’s face is always well-lit, with one side slightly shinier than the other
  2. with a few shiny spots on the forehead and the cheek
  3. There are no visible shadows on the face

  • Camera Mode: Manual

    Let’s do manual, because it sounds cool.. haha not exactly, because we want to make sure both the shutter speed and f number are exact.

  • Shutter Speed: 1/200

    It’s recommended that you always keep the shuttle speed around 1/200, which will always allow you to take crisp, clear photos, even if the person is moving slightly.

  • Aperture / f-number: 3.5

    Let’s set the f number to 3.5 instead of typically lower numbers(2.2, 2.5, 2.8), which will have a good depth of field effect and also allows more room for you to focus on the object.

  • ISO: Depends (Usually set to Auto)

    To compensate the brightness, you will need to adjust the ISO after you finish setting up the lighting. Please check the examples of brightness for reference.
